# Novel vector paradigms (LEANN, Turbovec, sqlite-vec, Lance) [vector-store-novel]

## SOTA SUMMARY
As of June 2026 the embedded-vector landscape has split into three viable families for a CPU-only, 8GB, 14.4M-vector desktop. (1) Disk-resident graph/IVF indexes with on-disk quantized vectors — DiskANN/Vamana and LanceDB's IVF_PQ — keep RAM bounded by holding compressed codes in memory and full/quantized vectors on SSD; this is the proven path to billion-scale on a single 64GB box (DiskANN: >95% recall@1, <5ms with GPU/server I/O) and degrades gracefully to our scale. (2) Storage-minimal recompute indexes — LEANN (MLSys 2026, 12.4k stars) — throw away stored embeddings and recompute ~O(log N) of them per query, hitting ~97% storage savings, but its published latencies (2.5-7.1s/query) are on an RTX 4090 and it uses GPU dynamic-batching; on a 4-core CPU with Qwen3-0.6B (~30ms/embed best-case on a 14-core i9, realistically 100-300ms on our 4-core target) a single query would recompute 25-30 vectors = multiple seconds, which violates PathKeep's no-freeze fluidity bar unless offloaded and heavily mitigated. (3) sqlite-vec is now at stable v0.1.9 (Mar 2026) but is still brute-force only in stable; its ANN (DiskANN/IVF) work remains alpha/experimental and not enabled, so it does not scale to 14.4M today, and it is a loadable extension — usable only in a SEPARATE sidecar DB, never the canonical one. The pragmatic 2026 consensus for local-first desktops at this scale is a Rust-native quantized ANN index in a rebuildable sidecar (usearch or LanceDB/Lance, or a pure-Rust HNSW like hnswlib-rs), with PQ/int8 to fit RAM, and treating LEANN-style recompute as an interesting-but-not-yet-CPU-ready research direction. Turbovec (May 2026, 3.5k stars, solo dev) is too new/low-trust to adopt now despite a credible Google-Research quantizer underneath.

## TOP PICKS: usearch (Rust crate, quantized HNSW + disk-view, rebuildable sidecar), LanceDB / Lance (Rust-native, disk-first IVF_PQ + on-disk re-rank, rebuildable sidecar), hnswlib-rs (pure-Rust, graph/vector decoupling enabling a quantized-or-recompute hybrid), LEANN (recompute paradigm — deep-dive ONLY to quantify CPU-only feasibility and a hot-cache hybrid)
